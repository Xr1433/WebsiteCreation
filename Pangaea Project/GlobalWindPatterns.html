Global Wind Patterns

What is Wind?

First off before we get into Wind Patterns you should probably know what wind is. Wind is a force of air created by a process called uneven heating. This uneven heating happens when air above land is warmer than air obove water.


Wind Patterns

Uneven heating produces global circulation patterns. When so much energy reachs the equator it starts to produces hot humid air that goes far up into the atmosphere. A low pressure area forms at the surface and a region of clouds forms at altitude. The air eventually stops rising and spreads north and south towards the Earth's poles. So far from where it started at the equator, the air falls back to Earth's surface puching towards the pole and then back to the equator. There are Six large convection currents around Earth from pole to pole.
